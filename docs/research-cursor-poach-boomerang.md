## What Happened

In early July 2025, Anysphere — the $500M ARR startup behind Cursor — recruited Boris Cherny and Cat Wu away from Anthropic with senior leadership titles. Cherny was offered Chief Architect and Head of Engineering. Wu was offered Head of Product.

Fourteen days later, both were back at Anthropic.

The speed of the reversal turned it into an industry spectacle. One observer compared it to "when LeBron went back to the Cavs but for nerds." More importantly, it surfaced something the market hadn't fully priced in: Claude Code's strategic weight inside Anthropic was enormous — large enough that the company moved fast enough to reverse a done deal in two weeks.

---

## Why It Matters

### 1. Cursor Validated Claude Code by Trying to Gut It

Anysphere didn't hire two random engineers. They targeted the architect and the product mind behind the specific product eating their market from the inside. Cursor *runs on Anthropic's models* — it's one of Anthropic's largest customers. The poach was an attempt to absorb the brain of the product that was making their own wrapper increasingly redundant.

The subtext: if your biggest customer needs to hire your lead engineer to compete, your product is the moat — not theirs.

### 2. The "80% of Anthropic's Code" Datapoint

Cherny stated publicly that approximately 80% of Anthropic's internal code is written by Claude. This is the lead engineer of Claude Code saying that the tool he built is responsible for the vast majority of the parent company's own engineering output. The implications cascade:

- Claude Code isn't a side project — it's the engine room
- The product eats its own tail: Claude writes Claude, and the person who architected that loop is the single highest-leverage hire in AI tooling
- Losing Cherny wasn't losing an engineer — it was losing the person who understood how to make AI write its own infrastructure

### 3. Anthropic's Counter-Speed Reveals Internal Valuation

Companies don't reverse poaches in fourteen days unless the departing talent represents existential value. The speed implies Anthropic's leadership viewed the Claude Code team as non-substitutable — not "we'll backfill in Q3" but "fix this now."

Context: at the time of the boomerang, investors were circling Anthropic at $100B+ valuations with improving gross margins. Losing Claude Code's architects during that fundraising window would have been a material narrative problem.

### 4. The Wrapper Problem Crystallizes

This incident is the sharpest illustration of the "wrapper vs. platform" tension in AI tooling:

- **Cursor** builds a polished IDE experience *on top of* Anthropic's models
- **Claude Code** is the model company's own first-party coding agent
- When the model improves, Claude Code improves for free. Cursor has to re-integrate, re-test, re-ship

Cursor poaching Claude Code's team was an implicit admission that the wrapper layer alone wasn't defensible. They needed the people who understood the model's capabilities at the deepest level — people who would always have earlier, deeper access at Anthropic than at a downstream customer.

---

## The Broader Signal

The AI coding tool market in mid-2025 operated under an assumption: Cursor, Windsurf, and other IDE wrappers would own the developer experience layer while model providers stayed upstream. The Cherny-Wu boomerang challenged that cleanly.

Anthropic isn't just providing the engine. With Claude Code, they're building the car — and hiring back the people who designed the steering.

For anyone building on top of a model provider's capabilities: the platform can always move down-stack. The question is when, not if.
